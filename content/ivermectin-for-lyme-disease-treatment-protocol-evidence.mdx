Ivermectin is a drug often used to kill [parasites](/medical-uses/ivermectin-for-parasites-types-treated-and-effectiveness "Ivermectin for Parasites: Types Treated and Effectiveness"). Recently, some have looked at it as a possible treatment for Lyme disease. Lyme is caused by a bacteria called Borrelia burgdorferi. It can be hard to find and treat. Because of this, some people are interested in new ways to fight the disease.

The idea to use ivermectin for Lyme comes from its ability to kill microbes. **Ivermectin** works by attaching to specific channels in [parasites](/medical-uses/ivermectin-for-parasites-types-treated-and-effectiveness), which makes them stop moving and die. Some lab studies have shown that ivermectin can also act against some bacteria, including Borrelia. This gives hope that it could help treat Lyme. 

Lyme often comes with other infections from parasites or bacteria. This makes treatment more hard. **Ivermectin**'s effects on parasites might help with these other infections too, and could lead to better results.

The research on ivermectin and Lyme is still new. There are few studies so far. But some small reports and tests have been done. These early studies give some clues about how ivermectin might work.

Some tests in the lab have shown that ivermectin can slow or stop the growth of Borrelia bacteria. These results show it might be useful. Still, what works in the lab does not always work in real patients. More research is needed.

There are no large, well-run studies yet that test how well ivermectin treats Lyme. Some patients have said they got better after using it. They reported less fatigue, joint pain, and nerve problems. But these are just reports, not proof.

Right now, there are no clear rules for how to use ivermectin for Lyme. Doctors who treat these patients often make their own plans. They try to match doses to each person. They may also give other medicines along with ivermectin.

It is very important to follow the instructions of a doctor when taking ivermectin. Patients should tell their doctor about any [side effects](/safety-side-effects/ivermectin-side-effects-complete-safety-guide "Ivermectin Side Effects: Complete Safety Guide") right away.

Some doctors are interested in using ivermectin with standard antibiotics like doxycycline or amoxicillin. These drugs attack Lyme in a different way. Using both might give a stronger fight against the disease.

When using ivermectin with antibiotics, doctors must watch for problems. They check liver function and look for **[side effects](/safety-side-effects/ivermectin-side-effects-complete-safety-guide)**. This helps keep the patient safe.

Many patients have shared their stories after using ivermectin for Lyme. Some say they feel a lot better. They report less tiredness, less muscle pain, and better thinking. But not everyone has the same results.

Some patients have **side effects**. These include nausea, dizziness, or upset stomach. Because of this, the use of ivermectin should be guided by a doctor.

The usual way to treat Lyme is with antibiotics. Doxycycline and amoxicillin are common choices. They are well tested and work well if given early. 

**Ivermectin** is not part of the main plan now. Its use is still new and experimental. It is usually tried if other treatments do not work or in cases where symptoms last a long time.
